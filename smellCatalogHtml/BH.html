Broken Hierarchy

This smell arises when a supertype and its subtype conceptually do not share an "IS-A" relationship resulting in broken substitutability.

This smell is also known as "Inappropriate use of inheritance" [Budd], "Containment by inheritance" [TrifuA], "Mistaken aggregates" [Page], "Misapplying IS A" [Page], "Improper inheritance" [Miller], "Inverse abstraction hierarchies" [Miller], "Subclasses do not redefine methods" [Lippert], and "Subclass inheriting inappropriate operations from superclass" [Page].

Reference

Girish Suryanarayana, Ganesh Samarthyam, and Tushar Sharma, Refactoring for Software Design Smells: Managing Technical Debt, 1st ed. Morgan Kaufmann, 2014.
