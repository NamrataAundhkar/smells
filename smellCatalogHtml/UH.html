Unnecessary Hierarchy

This smell arises when the whole inheritance hierarchy is unnecessary, indicating that inheritance has been applied needlessly for the particular design context.

This is also known as "Taxomania" [MeyerB] and "Object classes" [Llano].

Reference

Girish Suryanarayana, Ganesh Samarthyam, and Tushar Sharma, Refactoring for Software Design Smells: Managing Technical Debt, 1st ed. Morgan Kaufmann, 2014.
