A Taxonomy of Software Smells

Kent Beck coined the term "code smell" in the popular Refactoring book by Martin Fowler and defined it informally as certain structures in the code that suggest (sometimes they scream for) the possibility of refactoring. Since then, various smells have been reported that impair software quality in one or more ways. In this attempt, I present all the smell definitions present in existing literature with their references.
