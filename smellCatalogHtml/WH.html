Wide Hierarchy

This smell arises when an inheritance hierarchy is "too" wide indicating that intermediate types may be missing.

This smell is also known as "Wide inheritance hierarchy" [Semmle], "Missing levels of abstraction" [Miller], "Coarse hierarchies" [Miller], and "Getting away from abstraction" [Choinzon].

Reference

Girish Suryanarayana, Ganesh Samarthyam, and Tushar Sharma, Refactoring for Software Design Smells: Managing Technical Debt, 1st ed. Morgan Kaufmann, 2014.
